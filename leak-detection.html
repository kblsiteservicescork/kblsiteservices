Air leak detection

The cold isn't coming from your windows.

Heat escapes through gaps you'd never think to check. An air tightness test finds them, so you stop paying to heat the street.

Where leaks actually happen

Most homeowners assume drafts come from doors and windows. They rarely do. The real culprits are the joints and penetrations built into every house, the places nobody insulates because nobody sees them.

  • Under skirting boards, where the wall meets the floor
  • Through and behind integrated kitchen units
  • Behind bath panels and around plumbing
  • Around structural service pipes and cable runs
  • At attic hatches, service voids and floor-to-wall joints

What the test tells you

We pressurise the house with a blower door fan to simulate a windy day, then use thermal imaging and smoke to trace every path cold air takes through the building fabric. The result is a measured, visible map of your home's leaks, not an opinion.

This is the part that saves you money. Knowing exactly where the leaks are means you seal the right things. No five-figure insulation overhaul to fix a problem that turned out to be a €40 gap behind the bath. The survey pays for itself by stopping you spending on the wrong works.

What you walk away with

A clear, prioritised report. Hand it to a tradesperson and they know precisely what to do. Tackle it yourself and you have a step-by-step list ranked by impact. Either way, you're acting on evidence instead of guesswork.
