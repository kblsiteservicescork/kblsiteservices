How it works

What happens on test day.

The test takes around two hours on site. Here is what to expect.

01

We set up the blower door

The fan unit is sealed into a doorframe and all intentional openings — vents, trickle vents, fireplace flues — are temporarily sealed. Takes around 20 minutes.

02

We pressurise the building

The fan runs at multiple pressure levels to measure air flow. The result is an air permeability figure in m³/hr/m² — the number that appears on your certificate.

03

We locate the leaks

While the building is under pressure, we use thermal imaging and smoke to trace exactly where air is moving. Every gap is logged and photographed.

04

You get the results

Builders get their Part L certificate issued same day. Homeowners get a written report showing what to seal and in what order.
