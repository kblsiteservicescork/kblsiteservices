Air tightness testing

The number that proves your build performs.

Air tightness testing measures how much air leaks through the building fabric. It's a legal requirement for new builds under Part L and a critical baseline for any serious retrofit.

What air tightness actually measures

Every building has gaps — around pipes, cables, junctions between walls and floors, attic hatches, window reveals. The air permeability test quantifies exactly how much air escapes through those gaps at a standard pressure. The result is expressed as m³/hr/m² and must meet the target set out in the building regulations for your project type.

For new builds in Ireland, the maximum permitted is 7 m³/hr/m² under Part L. Most energy-efficient builds aim for 3 or below. Deep retrofits targeting BER upgrades need to demonstrate measurable improvement.

Fail on test day and you cannot sign off. The most common reason projects fail is penetrations that weren't sealed at pre-plaster stage — around soil pipes, cable runs and structural beams. We flag these during the test so they can be addressed before re-test.

New builds

We carry out tests at pre-completion stage. The blower door is set up, the building is pressurised and the result is calculated on site. If the result passes, your Part L certificate is issued the same day. If it doesn't, we identify exactly where the failures are so your team can seal them before the re-test.

Retrofits & energy upgrades

Deep retrofit schemes — including SEAI grants — require a pre-works and post-works air tightness test to demonstrate improvement. We carry out both, produce the required documentation and liaise with scheme administrators where needed.

Homeowners

If your house is cold, drafty or expensive to heat, an air tightness test tells you why. We test the building, locate the leaks and give you a prioritised report. You know exactly what to seal before spending on insulation or other works.
