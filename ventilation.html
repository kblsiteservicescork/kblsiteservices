Ventilation validation

Build tight. Ventilate right.

The tighter a building, the more its ventilation has to work. We test that it does — for new builds requiring Part F sign-off and for homes where sealing has made the air quality worse, not better.

Why ventilation can't be assumed

When a building is sealed tightly — whether through construction design or retrofit insulation works — the natural air movement that previously flushed out moisture, CO₂ and cooking humidity is reduced or eliminated. If the mechanical ventilation doesn't compensate, you end up with condensation, mould and poor air quality.

This is not a theoretical risk. It's the most common consequence of airtightness work done without a corresponding ventilation check.

For builders and contractors

Part F of the Irish building regulations requires that ventilation systems in new builds and significant retrofits are tested and certified. We measure actual airflow rates at each extract point, compare them against the design specification and issue the documentation needed for sign-off.

  • Extract fan flow measurement at each terminal
  • Comparison against Part F minimum requirements
  • MVHR and MEV system commissioning checks
  • Certification issued on completion

For homeowners

If your home has been insulated, had windows replaced or undergone any significant airtightness improvement, the ventilation should have been checked at the same time. If it wasn't, there is a risk of damp, black mould and stale air — none of which are cleaning problems. They are airflow problems.

Black mould is a symptom, not the cause. It comes back because the air carrying moisture has nowhere to go. Fix the airflow and it stops. We identify exactly which extract points are underperforming and what needs adjusting.

Independent, nothing to sell

We don't supply or install ventilation systems. We test what's there, tell you whether it works and document it. That independence matters — a company that sells systems has a reason to tell you the existing one isn't good enough. We don't.
